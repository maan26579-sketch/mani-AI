Here are the latest verified figures and summaries on the Russia-Ukraine conflict as of May 16, 2026, with authoritative sources:

---

### Civilian Casualties and Deaths
- The Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR) has verified, as of March 31, 2026:
  - **Total civilian casualties:** 58,930 (13,883 killed and 43,352 injured)
  - OHCHR notes the real numbers may be considerably higher due to limited access to some areas[[1]](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1293492/ukraine-war-casualties/)[[2]](https://thehuman-rights.com/ukraine-war-zone-civilian-casualties-displacement/).

- Since the full invasion in February 2022, cumulative OHCHR figures record:
  - **Over 15,000 civilians killed and more than 41,000 injured** by February 2026[[3]](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/four-years-full-scale-invasion-ukraine-key-facts-and-findings-february-2026)[[2]](https://thehuman-rights.com/ukraine-war-zone-civilian-casualties-displacement/).
  - At least 766 children killed and over 2,500 injured.
  - Civilian casualties occurred in 26 of Ukraine’s 27 administrative regions.

---

### Number of Refugees Abroad
- **Estimated Ukrainian refugees abroad:** 6.7 million as of early 2026 (UNHCR estimate).
- These refugees are mainly in Europe, with the largest populations in Poland, Germany, Czechia, and other EU countries[[2]](https://thehuman-rights.com/ukraine-war-zone-civilian-casualties-displacement/).

---

### Internally Displaced Persons (IDPs) Within Ukraine
- **Number of IDPs inside Ukraine:** about 6.5 million as of 2026.
- Many have faced multiple displacements and continue to face access challenges due to ongoing hostilities and attacks on infrastructure[[2]](https://thehuman-rights.com/ukraine-war-zone-civilian-casualties-displacement/).

---

### Major International Sanctions on Russia (Key Types & Dates)
**Sanctions Summary:**
- The international community, especially the EU, US, UK, Canada, Japan, and other allies, have imposed the broadest and most extensive sanctions ever on Russia since February 2022:
  - **Financial sector:** Removal of key Russian banks from SWIFT (Feb/March 2022); major asset freezes; restrictions on Russian Central Bank.
  - **Energy sector:** Phased bans or sharp reductions in Russian oil, coal, and gas imports; price caps on seaborne oil (from Dec 2022 onwards); end of most oil imports by EU by December 2022.
  - **Export controls:** Bans and restrictions on technology, military goods, aircraft, automotive and high-tech equipment exports to Russia; ongoing tightening through 2025 and 2026.
  - **Trade & transport:** Airspace and port closures to Russian vessels and aircraft (from early 2022); bans on exports of luxury goods and key raw materials.
  - **Individual designations:** Sanctions on President Vladimir Putin, senior officials, oligarchs, military officers, propagandists, and their families.
  - **Frozen assets:** Over $300 billion in Russian central bank assets frozen (2022). G7 discussions in 2026 on potential re-purposing of frozen assets for Ukraine's reconstruction are ongoing.
  - **Expansion/renewal:** Sanctions continue to be expanded and renewed at regular intervals, with the most recent EU 14th sanctions package announced in spring 2026.
- Source: EU Sanctions Map, US Treasury, UN briefings, and Al Jazeera report from February 2026[[4]]](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/23/the-ukraine-war-in-numbers-people-territory-money).
